Part 2: Simulation via numerical integration

Experiment with some the parameters in the simulation. To do so, pause the simulation at the start with P, modify the values of interest, and then resume by pressing P again. You can also restart the simulation at any time from the cloth's starting position by pressing R.
Describe the effects of changing the spring constant ks; how does the cloth behave from start to rest with a very low ks? A high ks?

From start to rest, the cloth ripples and then settles. Low ks = Looser overall and saggier at the top. High ks = Tighter overall and less sag at the top.


What about for density?

From start to rest, the cloth is saggier at the top with high density and barely sags with low density. The cloth falls at the same speed. Low density = Less ripples at the top. High density = More ripples at the top.


What about for damping?

From start to rest, the cloth falls slower and settles easier when damping is high and falls quickly and swings back and forth for a while before settling. Low damping = Lighter. High damping = Heavier.

Part 3: Handling collisions with other objects

Show us screenshots of your shaded cloth from scene/sphere.json in its final resting state on the sphere using the default ks = 5000 as well as with ks = 500 and ks = 50000. Describe the differences in the results.

With ks=5000, the cloth falls on the sphere regularly as expected. With ks=500, the cloth falls more limply to cover more of the sphere because the springs are looser. With ks=50000, the cloth falls more stiffly on the sphere and is slowly sliding off of the sphere because the springs are stiffer.
